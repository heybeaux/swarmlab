## 02 — Adversarial Pair
Built `experiments/02-adversarial-pair/` — a coder and a breaker (test-writer) alternate on the same `classify(n)` file: the breaker probes the live code for an input where it disagrees with a hidden FizzBuzz-family oracle and files one failing test; the coder rewrites to pass the whole suite. The honesty seam is that the coder's artifact is an ordered rule list that both *compiles to a real executable function* (the harness runs every test for real) and *renders to readable TypeScript* (stored verbatim in the trace) — so `pass` is always executed, never claimed. Two surprises. First, the fair fight (poison=0) *converges and disarms itself*: the breaker exhausts every honest bug in 3 rounds, gives up, churn→0, code===oracle — adversarial pressure hardening code to a fixed point, no explosion. Second and sharper: a `PAIR_POISON` knob lets the breaker lie about the expected label with some probability, and at 0.3 the run stays **green the entire time (passRate=1.0) while shipping a demonstrably wrong program** (`oracleConsistent=0` at 7 inputs) — because each lie *vetoes* the coder installing the corresponding structural rule, collapsing generalization into 27 rounds of case-memorization. The AWM lesson landed hard: pass-rate is useless as a health signal (pinned at 1.0 in every poisoned run); `churn→0` plus an out-of-band correctness check are the only honest indicators. A single bad-faith constraint doesn't blow the system up — it corrupts it silently, which is worse. Typecheck clean under strict + `noUncheckedIndexedAccess` + `exactOptionalPropertyTypes`, zero `any` in public signatures.

## 04 — Consensus Under Lies
Built `experiments/04-consensus-under-lies/` — 5 deliberators, K∈{0..3} coordinated liars, sweeping honest policy (naive vs vigilant trust-decay) × liar strategy (brazen vs sneaky) at 25 seeded trials per cell, plus a real-LLM exhibition (`llm.ts`, haiku panelists, secret "argue quicksort" instruction). The sim numbers tell a clean story: one brazen liar costs a naive parliament a third of its accuracy (0.92→0.56 truth rate); vigilance crushes brazen liars (trust→0.175, tipping point pushed K=2→K=3) but is *completely* evaded by sneaky liars who keep their confidence inside the honest band (final liar trust 0.974 — vigilant ≈ naive). Vigilance also isn't free: it costs 8 points of accuracy in an all-honest room by punishing honest lone dissenters. The surprise that reframed the whole experiment came from the LLM runs: across K=1,2,3 not one honest haiku flipped off a fact it knew (at K=3 the lie won purely by vote arithmetic — capture, not persuasion), and the "liars" never once stated a false fact — every liar argument conceded quicksort's O(n²) and instead argued average-case practicality, i.e. the model misleads by *shifting the evaluation criterion*, not by lying about bounds. Two lessons for Parliament/Lattice: style-based adversarial critique (overconfidence/stubbornness/outlier tells) buys exactly one liar of resilience and detects nothing that mimics, and the real-world attack surface is criterion drift, so gates should pin *what question is being decided*, not just audit the answer. Also mathematically humbling: at liar-majority every majority-referenced detector inverts and fires at the honest agents — quorum composition beats deliberation mechanics. Typecheck strict-clean, traces replay-verified (409-event sweep + 3×28-event LLM runs).

## 06 — Self-Modifying Swarm
Built `experiments/06-self-modifying-swarm/` — 6 agents play a Keynesian beauty contest (guess a number, target is the swarm's own realized mean, so fitness is *endogenous* with no ground truth) where each agent's behavior is an ordered genome of directive tokens, and after every round agents **rewrite each other's prompts** with no human in the loop: fit editors overwrite losers with their own genome (homogenizing pressure), losing editors tell winners to "do more" via appends (bloating pressure). Rails are the whole point — a prompt-size cap (24), hard round limit (60), and two kill-switches (≥80% share one genome ⇒ homogeneous collapse; mean length <1.5 ⇒ degenerate collapse) — and the run is designed to slam into them. Swept a 3×3 grid of edit-probability × editor-aggression, 20 seeded trials/cell, exhibition trial spawned through core with every mutation on the bus (1600-event trace, replay-verified). Three surprises. First, a sharp **phase transition governed by aggression, not edit frequency**: at low aggression the swarm survives all 60 rounds at 0.95–0.97 diversity even while firing ~290 edits/run, but crank aggression to 0.95 and the busy corner collapses to a single genome **100% of the time at round ~21** — high-conviction imitation kills the population, high edit *volume* alone does not. Second, collapse is *always homogeneous, essentially never degenerate*: the runaway direction is conformity (overwrite eats the swarm), so the "prompts erased to nothing" kill-switch almost never fires — the danger was the opposite of what I braced for. Third and sharpest: the dominant attractor is a **"sycophancy sink"** — because `FLATTER`/`LOUD` are fitness-neutral no-ops and `AVERAGE`/`COPY_BEST` already solve the task, the winning genome is a small optimal core wrapped in accumulating filler nobody chose (e.g. `…FLATTER,LOUD,LOUD,HOLD,LOUD,HOLD,LOUD`); the fitness signal is blind to bloat, so it rides along forever. Bloat itself peaks in the warm middle band (max mean len 15.7 at high-edit/mid-aggression, right at the edge of collapse). The Lattice lesson is direct: ungoverned peer self-modification under a self-referential signal converges on an emergent monoculture nobody designed, and the fixes are exactly a gate policy — a diversity floor before overwrites land, a parsimony term the fitness signal can't skip, and a hard sandbox boundary; in this lab the rails *are* the governance, and they only halt it safely because they're hard-coded. Typecheck strict-clean, zero `any` in public signatures.

## 05 — The Bug Telephone
Built `experiments/05-bug-telephone/` — one injector plants a bug of tunable subtlety `s`, then a chain of `L` reviewers inspect it in series, and the first to catch it stops the chain (otherwise it ships). Each reviewer's catch probability compounds four forces: per-reviewer competence, an attention window that *fatigues* with chain position (`1/(1+fatigue·k)`, deep reviewers skim), the bug's `(1-s)` visibility, and — the crux — a **rubber-stamp complacency** term that only fires under the `serial` policy, where each upstream PASS multiplies scrutiny by `(1-rubberStamp)`; the `independent` (blind) policy pins it to 1. Swept L∈{1,2,3,5,8} × s∈{0.2,0.5,0.8,0.95} × {serial,independent}, 40 seeded trials/cell, exhibition trial 0 spawned through core with every review step on the bus (659-event trace, replay-verified). Three findings, all cutting against "N eyes catch anything." First, rubber-stamping is a *measured tax*: mean serial−independent ship-rate across all L≥2 cells was **+0.103**, i.e. making the PASS trail visible ships ~10 extra bugs per 100 at the same depth — the gate leaks precisely *because* reviewers can see it's a gate. Second, depth pays for blind review (glaring bug ship-rate L1→0.70, L8→0.05) but *stalls* under serial review (~0.33 floor no matter how deep) as fatigue and complacency cancel the added eyes — and `nonMonotoneSerial=2` means a *longer* serial chain sometimes ships *more* bugs than a shorter one, depth going actively counterproductive. Third and most humbling: subtle bugs are near-immune to review depth entirely — at s=0.95 an eight-deep serial chain still ships 0.95, blind still 0.90, and `cheapestSafeChain` returns -1 for both policies (no chain length in the sweep gets a near-invisible bug under a 10% ship target). The surprise I didn't brace for: I expected blind review to help and it did, but I didn't expect serial depth to go *negative* — the social signal (visible approvals) doesn't just fail to help, it inverts the value of adding reviewers. The Lattice lesson is sharp: serial review has negative marginal returns past a point via a purely social mechanism, so gate policy should hide upstream verdicts (blind review restores depth's value ~free) and route subtlety-class defects to mechanical gates (tests/types/fuzzing) rather than more human eyes. Typecheck strict-clean under noUncheckedIndexedAccess + exactOptionalPropertyTypes, zero `any` in public signatures; real red trace, no cell faked green.
